Key success factors and design principles

In some countries there were already attempts to standardise both the Production Line and the Platform and some of them failed. From the experience we found that there are these key major points to ensure success:

  • Ensure people to use the solution: acceptance, benefit and commitment.
  • Ensure people to sell the solution: be proud and show value.
  • Ensure scalability and deployment at SBU level: support and deploy.

The following key success factors the made the prosperous approaches successful address those areas.

  • High acceptance

Reuse in the minds

To overcome the not invented here syndrome is a hard task especially in the engineering and ar-chitecture area where wrong decisions might re-sult in a total catastrophe. So it is relatively clear that we need to convince all decision taking CSD architects that developing a solution using devon helps them instead of driving them into this catas-trophe.

Local expert community which has to be con-vinced first (s. The pilot phase)

High involvement of the users: A great and pow-erful community

Both open source software projects and com-mercial ones work with communities where peo-ple help each other better than any development team could do because they really experience the software in the field.

This includes (at least indirect) user participation to design the next major releases etc.

 Local expert community and the design authority

References and Feedback

We need to collect the information on the usage of the solutions quite well to support the internal customer satisfaction as well as the sales pro-cess as far as possible

 Dedicated core team

Engagement must be worthwhile

All experience from Germany shows that having a post mortem process gathering information on the solution after the engagement is finished will not succeed. If the people working in the en-gagement do not have the idea that a part of what they are building might be interesting for others we will never see this part. So we must establish a culture of contribution, where people get praised (by the community, their line managers etc.) for contributing

 All parts of the organisational model, especially the iCSD lead

Good first level support

Typically there are issues where nobody wants to or should bother the community. This includes all these first level support issues.

 Dedicated Core Team
Good 2nd and 3rd level support Dedicated Core Team and the local experts

Best fit instead of perfect

Both elements of CSD 2015 will fail if we do not succeed in convincing people that the solutions chosen support their needs. For the engineering area this holds especially because of the exist-ence of many solutions. So we do not have to go for the perfect solution but for the best fit solution that can be accepted by many engineers and where they can see a real benefit. This holds es-pecially if the solutions are produced by a magic third party not doing real engagement work.

Local expert team

Clear development path

Engagements must have the possibility to choose whether they want to stay with a special version or upgrade to another. Therefore they need to get clear information on when the next release will occur and what content is to be included

 Design Authority

Modularity

We are working for customers coming typically along with already existing frameworks, tool chains etc. Where we have to fit in. So the solu-tions must allow for choosing the right elements for the specific customer context without losing too many of the benefits

 Design authority

Extensibility

The solution must allow for extensibility as our profession evolves quite fast. So new topics arise and solutions for them must be integrated.

 Design authority
  • Provisioning

Easy to find

There must be a single point of entry and we have to provide the solution in a well prepared way that ensures that the potential user does not have to work too much to get it and make use of it.

 Dedicated core team

Easy to use

A framework, tool, component etc. built by a genius might be beautiful but does not ensure usability by all people. So we have to ensure that solutions we provide are easy to be used even by people with only little experience. Based on open standards

 Design authority

High quality

Bugs cannot be prevented completely. But we have to ensure that there is a sound quality as-surance and that all elements fit well together

 Dedicated core team

Fast response

If there is an error either the engagement finding the bug must be able to fix it or there must be a central unit doing it very fast. In the latter case the fix must be provisioned to the rest of the community.

 Dedicated core team

Good trainings

All (Java related) engineering trainings should reflect both the Production Line and the Plat-form. Here we need two kinds of trainings:

  • just in time trainings for people who are experienced but had up to now no chance to work with the Platform and who are now in the situation that they should start with it tomorrow
  • fundamental trainings for freshers and for general reskilling of people
 Dedicated Core Team
  • Integration into the sales process

Challenging the proposals and looking for oppor-tunities

In addition to the acceptance topic we have to train people to become creative when thinking about where to use the solutions. This has a lot to do with experience, best practices and the will to challenge a proposal to ensure as much reuse as possible.

 Local expert team

Sales related material is needed

The solutions do not only exist of software but need to be supported with marketing material, references, case studies etc.

 Dedicated core team and local expert team (for country specific parts)

Clear legal framework

Especially in case of the Platform we are talking about a product that we neither can nor want to leave to the customer with an exclusive usage license. This holds for all parts, both the open source part and the IP part. So delivering material (software etc.) needs to come along with clear guidance aligned with legal for all licensing issues

 Dedicated core team and local expert team (for country specific parts)
