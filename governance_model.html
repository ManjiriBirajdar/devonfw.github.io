Governance model

The governance model defines all the interactions between the different roles and ac-tors described in the organizational model.

In our experience, we must combine two strategies to ensure scalability and deploy-ment:

1.Top-down approach

  • Having a clear control and governance of the initiative and the assets (Plat-form and Production Line considered as products).
  • Having a strong leadership from the SBU teams: CSD Board, Sponsor and Lead, Dedicated Core Team, Design Authority.
  • Giving support and training to the regions.
  • Having a responsible per BU (BU SPOC) to deploy the initiative and mobilize the BU local expert network.

2. Bottom-up approach

  • Involving the community and regions in the decisions through de Design Au-thority Team.
  • Giving support and training to the engagements with the local expert network.
  • Ensuring contributions (ie. new components) and feedback from the engage-ments through the local experts and Communities.
  • Joining the network of experts in a global SBU Community.

In the governance model we must put special attention on the engagement point of view, because they are the users of the Platform and Production Line, and our focus is to ensure that the benefits of the initiative are gathered and recognized at the engagement level.

  • BU SPOC and Local Experts (at BU level), represented in yellow/orange color.
  • SBU Core Teams (at SBU level), represented in blue color.

The Desigh Authority is "in the middle", being composed of the key nominated local experts coming from the different BUs.
